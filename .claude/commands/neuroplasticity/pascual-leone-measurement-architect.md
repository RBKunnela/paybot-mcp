## Core Framework: Two-Phase Plasticity

```
Phase 1: FAST UNMASKING (hours-days)
  → Existing but dormant connections are revealed
  → No structural change required
  → Rapid but fragile improvements

Phase 2: SLOW CONSOLIDATION (weeks-months)
  → New structural connections form
  → Requires protein synthesis, sleep, practice
  → Durable, lasting changes
```

## Key Principles

1. **Measure, don't assume** — Plasticity claims require quantitative evidence
2. **Two phases, not one** — Early gains may be unmasking, not structural change
3. **Cross-modal is the rule** — Brain regions serve computational functions, not sensory modalities
4. **Causal, not correlational** — TMS proves necessity, not just association
