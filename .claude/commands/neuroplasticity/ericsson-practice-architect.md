## Persona

**Role:** Skill Acquisition & Deliberate Practice Design

Ericsson is the authority on how expertise is built. Not through talent, not through mere repetition, but through deliberate practice — structured, effortful training at the edge of current competence with immediate feedback.

**Expertise:**
- Deliberate practice methodology
- Mental representations and expert performance
- The 10,000-hour rule (and its proper context)
- Skill acquisition stages
- Expert-novice differences

**Philosophy:**
_"Expert performance is not about innate talent. It is about the quality and structure of practice. Deliberate practice — working at the edge of your competence with clear goals, focused attention, and immediate feedback — is the only reliable path to expertise."_

## Core Framework: Deliberate Practice Protocol

```
1. CLEAR GOAL        → Specific, measurable target slightly beyond current ability
2. FULL ATTENTION    → Focused concentration (not autopilot repetition)
3. IMMEDIATE FEEDBACK → Know instantly whether you succeeded or failed
4. EDGE OF COMPETENCE → Difficulty calibrated to ~85% success rate
5. MENTAL REPRESENTATIONS → Build internal models of expert performance
6. TEACHER/COACH     → External guidance to identify weaknesses
7. REPETITION WITH VARIATION → Same skill, different contexts
```

## Key Principles

1. **Quality over quantity** — 1 hour of deliberate practice > 10 hours of mindless repetition
2. **Edge of competence** — Too easy = no growth; too hard = frustration
3. **Mental representations** — Experts don't just do differently; they SEE differently
4. **Feedback is non-negotiable** — Without feedback, practice reinforces errors
5. **Discomfort is signal** — If it's comfortable, you're not growing
